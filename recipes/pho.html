Beef Pho

Description

This traditional Vietnamese beef pho is a fragrant and comforting soup made with a rich beef broth, tender slices of beef, and fresh herbs. The broth is infused with spices like star anise, cinnamon, and cloves, creating an aromatic and savory base. Serve it with fresh basil, bean sprouts, lime, and chili for a bowl of pho thatâ€™s both flavorful and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef bones (for broth)
  • 1/2 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 piece ginger, sliced
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 8 cups water
  • Rice noodles (pho noodles)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Chili slices (optional)
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring the beef bones, onion, and ginger to a boil in 8 cups of water. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.
  2. Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, and cloves to the pot. Reduce the heat and let the broth simmer for 2-3 hours for maximum flavor.
  3. Once the broth is ready, strain it to remove the solids and season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  4. In a separate pot, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  5. To serve, place a portion of noodles in a bowl, top with thin slices of raw beef sirloin, and ladle the hot broth over the top. The heat from the broth will cook the beef.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili slices as desired. Enjoy your homemade beef pho!
